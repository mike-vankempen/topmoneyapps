The Best Mint Alternatives in 2026

Intuit shut down Mint in January 2024, leaving 3.6 million users without a budgeting tool. Here's where to go depending on what you valued about Mint:

  • If you loved Mint's free price: Switch to Empower Personal Dashboard — it's free and more powerful for tracking net worth and investments.
  • If you want the closest feature match: Monarch Money replicates Mint's interface almost exactly, with better reliability and collaborative access for couples.
  • If you want to actually change your habits: Mint was great at showing where money went. YNAB goes further — it helps you plan where it should go. The behavioral shift is worth the subscription.
  • If you're on Apple: Copilot Money is the upgrade you didn't know you needed — AI-powered, beautiful, and tightly integrated with the Apple ecosystem.

How to Choose the Right Budgeting App for You

Not every budgeting app works for every person. Here's a simple framework to find your match:

  • If you're in debt or paycheck-to-paycheck: YNAB. The zero-based method is specifically designed to break the cycle. The results are dramatic — and the trial is free for 34 days.
  • If you're financially comfortable but want oversight: Copilot or Simplifi. You don't need aggressive budgeting — just a clear picture of your spending.
  • If you're managing finances with a partner: Monarch Money. Collaborative access and shared budgets make it uniquely powerful for households.
  • If you just need something free: Empower. You'll get account aggregation, net worth tracking, and cash flow analysis without spending a dollar.
  • If you love spreadsheets: Tiller. Full control over your data with automatic bank import.
