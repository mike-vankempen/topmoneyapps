⚡ Our Verdict

Robinhood is the best starting point for new investors — zero commissions, $1 fractional shares, a clean interface, and the broadest asset access (stocks, ETFs, options, crypto, and IRAs) in one free app. It's not ideal for serious long-term investors (Fidelity is better) or advanced options traders (tastytrade wins there), but for the majority of beginners it's the right first app.

What Is Robinhood?

Robinhood launched in 2015 with a single mission: make investing accessible to everyone by eliminating trading commissions. It worked — the app has 24+ million users and fundamentally changed the brokerage industry, forcing every major broker to drop commissions to zero.

Today Robinhood offers stocks, ETFs, options, cryptocurrency, IRAs, and a cash management account, all in one app. The free tier is genuinely powerful, and the Gold membership ($6.99/month) adds 5% APY on cash, Morningstar research, and a 3% IRA match.

Key Features

Commission-Free Trading

Robinhood charges $0 commission on stock, ETF, and options trades. For options, there's no per-contract fee either — unlike most brokers that charge $0.65/contract. The only costs are regulatory fees (typically a few cents per trade) that all brokers pass through. Robinhood makes money through payment for order flow (PFOF) and Gold subscriptions.

Fractional Shares

Buy any stock or ETF for as little as $1. This means you can invest in high-priced stocks like Berkshire Hathaway or Amazon without needing thousands of dollars. Fractional shares are available for all S&P 500 stocks and many ETFs, making diversification affordable for any budget.

Robinhood Gold

Gold costs $6.99/month and adds significant value if you use the features:

  • 5% APY on uninvested cash (via sweep program, FDIC-insured up to $2.5M)
  • 3% IRA match on eligible contributions (vs 1% on free tier)
  • Morningstar research — professional equity reports on 1,700+ stocks
  • Level II market data from Nasdaq
  • Margin investing at 6% interest rate

IRA with 1–3% Match

Robinhood's IRA contribution match is a standout feature. The free tier gives a 1% match; Gold members get a 3% match on eligible contributions. On the 2026 IRA contribution limit of $7,000, that's $70–$210 in free money annually — a feature no other major brokerage offers at this scale.

Cryptocurrency

Robinhood offers ~20 cryptocurrencies including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Dogecoin, and other major coins. Trading is commission-free, but Robinhood makes money on the spread (difference between buy/sell price). Important limitation: you cannot withdraw crypto to an external wallet — Robinhood holds custody of your crypto. For true crypto ownership, use Coinbase instead.

Who Should Use Robinhood?

  • Beginner investors — the simplest interface and lowest barrier to entry of any major brokerage
  • Investors starting with small amounts — $1 fractional shares let you build a diversified portfolio immediately
  • People who want stocks + crypto in one app — most brokers force you to use separate apps
  • IRA savers — the 1–3% contribution match is unique and genuinely valuable over time
  • Cash holders — Robinhood Gold's 5% APY on uninvested cash is among the highest available

Who Should Use Something Else?

  • Long-term index fund investors — Fidelity offers FZROX at 0.00% expense ratio and better research tools
  • Serious options traders — tastytrade has a $10/leg commission cap and superior analytics
  • Advanced traders — Webull's extended hours (4AM–8PM) and 50+ technical indicators are better for active trading
  • Crypto investors who want true ownership — Coinbase lets you withdraw to your own wallet; Robinhood doesn't

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Robinhood safe in 2026?▼
Yes, Robinhood is safe. Securities are SIPC-insured up to $500,000. Robinhood Gold accounts include $2.5 million in FDIC insurance on uninvested cash. Robinhood is regulated by FINRA and the SEC. The app uses 2-factor authentication and biometric login.
Is Robinhood good for beginners?▼
Yes, Robinhood is one of the best apps for beginner investors. It has the simplest interface of any major brokerage, $1 fractional shares, zero commissions, and a clean mobile-first design. The downside is limited educational content — beginners should supplement with outside resources to learn investing fundamentals.
What is Robinhood Gold and is it worth it?▼
Robinhood Gold costs $6.99/month and adds 5% APY on uninvested cash, Morningstar research, Level II market data, margin investing at 6%, and a 3% IRA contribution match. It's worth it if you keep $1,000+ in uninvested cash — the 5% APY alone covers the monthly fee. For most beginners, the free tier is sufficient.
Does Robinhood have fractional shares?▼
Yes. Robinhood offers fractional shares starting at $1, so you can invest in any S&P 500 stock or major ETF with any amount. This makes Robinhood accessible to investors of all budget sizes and allows for easy dollar-cost averaging.
Robinhood vs Fidelity — which is better?▼
Robinhood is better for beginners who want a simple, mobile-first trading experience. Fidelity is better for long-term investors who want zero-expense-ratio index funds (FZROX at 0.00%), better research tools, and a full-service brokerage. Most serious investors start on Robinhood and eventually move to Fidelity or Schwab as their needs grow.
Does Robinhood have an IRA?▼
Yes. Robinhood offers both traditional IRA and Roth IRA accounts with a 1% contribution match on the free tier and 3% for Gold members. Contributing the 2026 IRA maximum of $7,000 earns $70–$210 in free matching contributions annually — a feature no other major brokerage matches.
