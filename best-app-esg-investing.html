Understanding ESG Investing

What Does ESG Actually Mean?

Environmental: Climate change mitigation, renewable energy, carbon emissions, pollution. Social: Fair wages, diversity/inclusion, labor practices, community impact. Governance: Board diversity, executive compensation, shareholder rights, ethics.

ESG vs. Socially Responsible vs. Impact Investing

ESG investing selects companies by E/S/G metrics. Socially Responsible (SRI) avoids "sin stocks" (tobacco, weapons, fossil fuels). Impact investing actively targets positive outcomes (clean energy, clean water, affordable housing).

Does ESG Really Matter for Performance?

Over 10 years, ESG funds matched or exceeded S&P 500 returns. During 2021-2023, ESG underperformed as energy stocks surged (ESG avoids oil/gas). This shows ESG has real diversification effects—sometimes it leads, sometimes it lags.

How to Identify Greenwashing

Some "ESG" funds are just marketing. Check for: (1) Third-party ESG ratings (MSCI, Sustainalytics), (2) Full holdings transparency, (3) Clear exclusion criteria, (4) Regular ESG reporting. Betterment, Wealthfront, and Fidelity all publish detailed ESG methodology.
