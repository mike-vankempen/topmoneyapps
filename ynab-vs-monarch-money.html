YNAB vs Monarch Money (2026): Best Mint Replacement?

📅 March 2026 ⏱ 9 min read ✍️ TopMoneyApps Editorial Team

Mint shut down in January 2024, displacing over 40 million users who suddenly needed a new budgeting app. Two apps quickly rose to the top of every "best Mint alternative" list: YNAB and Monarch Money. They both cost $14.99/month — but they work in fundamentally different ways and appeal to very different types of people. Here's exactly how to choose.

Quick Verdict: YNAB vs Monarch Money

Choose YNAB if you want to take real control of your spending, pay off debt, or break the paycheck-to-paycheck cycle. YNAB's zero-based budgeting method is genuinely life-changing for people who commit to it — but it requires active engagement and a learning curve of a few weeks.

Choose Monarch Money if you want an automatic, passive financial dashboard that tracks your spending, net worth, and investments in one place with minimal effort. It's the most direct Mint replacement — comprehensive, beautiful, and collaborative (great for couples).

⭐ Our Take

If you used Mint for passive tracking, Monarch Money is your best replacement. If you want to actively change your financial behavior and build savings discipline, YNAB will deliver more transformation. Both are among the best budgeting apps we've reviewed — the right choice depends entirely on whether you want passive oversight or active control.

YNAB Review: Best for Active Budgeters

YNAB (You Need A Budget) is built on a single, powerful philosophy: give every dollar a job before you spend it. This zero-based budgeting approach means you assign every dollar of income to a specific category — rent, groceries, eating out, savings — before the money gets spent. The result, for people who commit to the method, is often dramatic: YNAB claims new users save an average of $600 in their first two months and $6,000 in their first year.

What YNAB does best

  • Zero-based budgeting — the most effective budgeting method for people who want to actively change their financial behavior. Every dollar has a purpose.
  • Debt payoff tools — built-in debt payoff planner shows you exactly when you'll be debt-free and how to accelerate it.
  • 34-day free trial — the longest free trial of any paid budgeting app. More than enough time to determine if the method works for you.
  • Real-time sync — transactions sync from 12,000+ financial institutions. The mobile app lets you log cash transactions on the spot.
  • Age of Money metric — YNAB tracks how long money sits in your account before being spent. As you budget better, your "Age of Money" increases — a concrete measure of financial buffer.

YNAB drawbacks

  • Requires regular engagement — the budgeting method only works if you check in and adjust categories frequently.
  • Learning curve — most users need 2–3 weeks before the method feels natural. YNAB provides extensive tutorials but it's still a commitment.
  • Investment tracking is basic — YNAB is not designed to be a portfolio tracker or net worth dashboard.
  • No free tier — after the 34-day trial, you pay $14.99/month or $99/year.

Monarch Money Review: Best Mint Replacement

Monarch Money was founded in 2019 by former Intuit employees who saw Mint's weaknesses firsthand. When Mint shut down in early 2024, Monarch was ready — and immediately became the top-rated Mint alternative. Its comprehensive dashboard tracks spending, budgets, net worth, investment portfolios, and financial goals all in one view, with beautiful design and reliable bank connections.

What Monarch Money does best

  • Complete financial dashboard — spending, net worth, investments, and budgets all visible from a single screen. The closest experience to what Mint offered.
  • Couples and joint accounts — built from the ground up for partners to manage finances together. Both partners can view, edit, and comment on the same financial data.
  • Investment portfolio tracking — connects to brokerage accounts and displays asset allocation, performance, and portfolio value alongside your budget.
  • Net worth over time — tracks net worth historically so you can see your financial progress on a chart. Highly motivating.
  • Cashflow forecasting — projects future account balances based on scheduled income and bills, so you can see cash crunches coming.

Monarch Money drawbacks

  • Only a 7-day free trial — significantly shorter than YNAB's 34 days. Not much time to evaluate the full feature set.
  • Less powerful for active debt payoff — Monarch has budgeting tools but lacks YNAB's dedicated debt payoff planner.
  • Budgeting is passive by default — if you want to be pushed to change your spending behavior, Monarch is gentler than YNAB.

Price: Are They Worth $99/Year?

At $99/year ($8.25/month when billed annually), both apps are identically priced. The question is whether either is worth it. For YNAB, the math is straightforward: if you save even $200 more than you would have otherwise in a year, YNAB pays for itself twice over. Their internal data suggests new users save $600 in month one alone.

For Monarch Money, the value is more about visibility than behavior change. If you currently have money scattered across multiple banks and investment accounts with no clear picture of your net worth, Monarch's consolidation alone can be worth the price — you'll make better financial decisions when you can see everything clearly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is YNAB or Monarch Money a better Mint replacement?▼
Both are excellent Mint replacements, but for different users. Monarch Money is the more direct replacement — it tracks spending, budgets, net worth, and investments automatically in one dashboard, similar to Mint. YNAB is better if you want to actively control your spending through zero-based budgeting. If you used Mint passively for tracking, choose Monarch Money. If you want to break the paycheck-to-paycheck cycle, choose YNAB.
How much does YNAB cost vs Monarch Money in 2026?▼
YNAB costs $14.99/month or $99/year, with a 34-day free trial. Monarch Money also costs $14.99/month or $99/year, but only offers a 7-day free trial. The annual pricing is identical at $99/year for both apps as of 2026.
Is YNAB or Monarch Money better for couples?▼
Monarch Money is better for couples. It was built with joint financial management in mind — both partners can view, edit, and comment on shared budgets and accounts. YNAB can be shared on one subscription, but its zero-based budgeting approach works best when both partners are equally engaged, which can create friction if financial priorities differ.
Does YNAB track investments and net worth?▼
YNAB allows you to add investment accounts to calculate net worth, but investment tracking is not its primary focus — budgeting is. Monarch Money provides more comprehensive investment portfolio tracking including asset allocation breakdown, performance over time, and a net worth chart showing historical trends. For full financial visibility beyond just spending, Monarch Money is the stronger choice.
Which budgeting app is easier to use?▼
Monarch Money is easier to use for most people. Connect your accounts and it starts tracking automatically with minimal setup — similar to Mint. YNAB has a steeper learning curve: the zero-based budgeting method typically takes 2–3 weeks to feel natural. YNAB provides excellent tutorials, but it requires a real time investment upfront. The payoff in financial transformation can be greater with YNAB, but Monarch requires far less ongoing effort.
