YNAB Review: Best for Serious Budgeters

YNAB stands for "You Need A Budget." It's not just an app — it's a philosophy. Founded on the principle of zero-based budgeting (every dollar you earn is assigned a job), YNAB teaches you to be intentional about money. Rated 4.9/5 across 12,000+ reviews, YNAB has helped millions of people take control of their finances. Users report saving an average of $600 in month one and $6,000 in year one.

What YNAB does well

  • Zero-based budgeting methodology — every dollar is assigned a purpose before you spend it. This shifts your entire relationship with money.
  • Proven results — users save $600 avg in month 1 and $6,000 in year 1. Real, documented outcomes.
  • Works everywhere — iOS, Android, Web, and Desktop apps. Sync across all devices.
  • 34-day free trial — longer than Copilot. Test the methodology without committing.
  • Shared budgets — designed for couples. Up to 6 people can access one budget.
  • Educational content — YNAB's blog and videos teach financial principles, not just features.
  • Professional ratings — 4.9/5 across 12,000+ reviews. Trusted by financial experts.

YNAB drawbacks

  • Learning curve — zero-based budgeting is a philosophy. Takes time to understand and adopt.
  • More expensive — $14.99/month vs Copilot's $13/month (though this difference disappears with annual plans).
  • No investment tracking — doesn't track stocks, crypto, or investment accounts.
  • Requires intentionality — you need to actively categorize spending; Copilot's AI does it for you.

Copilot Review: Best for Apple Lovers Who Want Beauty

Copilot is a beautifully designed budgeting app for Apple users (iPhone, iPad, Mac). Instead of teaching methodology, Copilot uses AI to categorize your spending automatically, shows you net worth in real-time, and even tracks your investments. The interface is gorgeous — budgeting feels effortless. It's less about "learning to budget" and more about "automatically understanding your money."

What Copilot does well

  • Beautiful design — Copilot's interface is genuinely gorgeous. Budgeting feels easy and enjoyable.
  • AI categorization — automatically sorts transactions. You don't have to do it manually.
  • Net worth dashboard — see your total wealth at a glance (bank accounts, savings, investments).
  • Investment tracking — connects to stock brokerages and crypto exchanges. See your entire net worth in one place.
  • Insights and spending analysis — AI highlights trends and patterns in your spending.
  • Slightly cheaper — $13/month vs YNAB's $14.99/month.
  • Apple ecosystem optimization — seamless integration with iOS and macOS.

Copilot drawbacks

  • Apple only — no Android, Windows, or web app. If you ever switch to Android or need Windows access, you can't use Copilot.
  • No budgeting methodology — beautiful but passive. It doesn't teach you how to budget better.
  • Shorter free trial — only 14 days vs YNAB's 34 days.
  • Less proven — newer app with fewer long-term success stories compared to YNAB's documented results.
  • Requires intentionality too — even with AI, you still need to set spending goals and review regularly.

Pricing Compared

YNAB: $14.99/month (or $99/year = $8.25/month). Copilot: $13/month (or $95/year = $7.92/month). Annual plans make Copilot slightly cheaper. Both offer 14–34 day free trials.

The price difference is trivial. Choose based on platform needs and methodology, not cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Copilot better than YNAB?▼
Not objectively. Copilot has a more beautiful, modern interface and works great if you're an Apple user. YNAB has a proven zero-based budgeting methodology, is available on all platforms (iOS, Android, Web, Desktop), and has a 34-day free trial. Choose Copilot if you love Apple and design; choose YNAB if you want a proven methodology and platform flexibility.
Does Copilot work on Android?▼
No. Copilot is Apple ecosystem only — iOS (iPhone/iPad) and macOS (Mac). There is no Android, Windows, or web version of Copilot. If you need to budget on Android or Windows, YNAB is your only choice between these two.
YNAB vs Copilot for couples: which is better?▼
Both apps support multiple users and shared budgets. YNAB lets up to 6 people access one budget and is specifically designed for joint budgeting (splitting expenses, shared goals). Copilot also supports multiple users on one budget. Both work well for couples — choose based on whether you prefer zero-based methodology (YNAB) or AI-powered simplicity (Copilot).
Which is easier to learn: YNAB or Copilot?▼
Copilot is easier to learn visually — the beautiful design makes budgeting feel intuitive. YNAB has more of a learning curve because zero-based budgeting is a philosophy you need to understand. However, YNAB's 34-day free trial gives you a month to learn without paying. Both have free trials — Copilot 14 days, YNAB 34 days.
What's the difference between YNAB's free trial and Copilot's?▼
YNAB offers a 34-day free trial (over a month) with zero credit card required. You can use the full app without paying. Copilot offers a 14-day free trial. YNAB's longer trial is better if you want time to truly test the zero-based methodology. Copilot's shorter trial is still enough to explore the interface and AI features.
