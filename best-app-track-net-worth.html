Quick Answer

Empower is the best free net worth tracker — it connects every account type, shows investment fees, and projects retirement readiness at no cost. For couples or those who want budgeting alongside net worth, Monarch Money ($14.99/mo) is the top paid option. If you hold crypto, real estate, or alternative assets, Kubera ($15/mo) tracks them all in one place.

Empower (Personal Capital) Free Editor's Pick
Best overall free net worth tracker

Empower has over 3.7 million users and is the gold standard of free personal finance dashboards. Connect your checking accounts, savings, investment portfolios, 401(k)s, IRAs, mortgages, and loans — the dashboard calculates your net worth in real time and charts it over time.

The Investment Checkup tool is Empower's killer feature: it scans your portfolio, flags high-expense-ratio funds, and shows you how much those fees will cost over 20 years. The Retirement Planner lets you run Monte Carlo simulations of different savings scenarios. All of this is genuinely free.

The trade-off: Empower runs a paid wealth management service (0.89% AUM fee) and its advisors will reach out. These calls are easy to decline and don't affect the free dashboard.

Monarch Money built its reputation on clean design and being the best Mint replacement. For net worth specifically, it combines a real-time net worth dashboard, account aggregation via Plaid and Finicity, and the ability for two people to view shared finances with individual controls over what each partner sees.

The Net Worth Over Time chart breaks down asset growth vs. liability reduction so you can see which is driving your wealth. Custom account groups let you segment "Retirement", "Home Equity", and "Liquid Savings" separately. Monarch also lets you manually add assets like cars (KBB), real estate, and private investments that can't be synced.

At $99.99/year, a couple splitting the cost pays $50/year each — comparable to other finance apps but with far more features.

Kubera is the only net worth tracker built from the ground up for complex, diverse portfolios. Traditional brokerage and bank accounts are supported, but Kubera goes further: connect Ethereum wallets directly (it reads the blockchain), track DeFi protocol holdings, sync real estate values from Zillow, look up car values from KBB, and add private equity or angel investments manually.

Kubera also includes a "Beneficiary" feature where you can designate someone who gets access to your full financial picture in the event you become incapacitated — a unique and thoughtful feature for estate planning. It's niche, but for the right user it's the only tool that does the job.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free app to track net worth?
Empower is the best completely free net worth tracker. It connects banks, brokerages, 401(k)s, IRAs, and loans in one dashboard, analyzes investment fees, and projects retirement readiness — all at no cost. Over 3.7 million people use the free version.
What happened to Mint for net worth tracking?
Mint shut down in March 2024. The best Mint replacements for net worth tracking are Empower (free), Monarch Money ($14.99/mo), and Copilot Money ($13.99/mo). All three auto-sync accounts and show a live net worth number similar to what Mint offered.
Can I track crypto and real estate in a net worth app?
Kubera is the best option for alternative assets. It supports Ethereum wallet connections, DeFi protocol tracking, Zillow real estate estimates, and KBB car values — alongside traditional bank and brokerage accounts. Empower lets you manually add real estate and crypto holdings as well.
Is it safe to link bank accounts to net worth apps?
Yes, when using reputable apps. Empower, Monarch, Copilot, and YNAB all connect via Plaid or Finicity — read-only connections that cannot initiate transfers. All use 256-bit AES encryption. Your bank credentials are not stored by the app directly.
Which net worth app is best for couples?
Monarch Money is the top choice for couples — one subscription includes two user seats with shared dashboards and individual account controls. Copilot Money also supports household sharing. Empower and Kubera are designed for single users.
How often do these apps update your net worth?
Most apps sync every 24 hours automatically. Investment account values update at end of trading day. Empower and Monarch can refresh within a few hours of a transaction posting. You can trigger a manual refresh in any of these apps at any time.
