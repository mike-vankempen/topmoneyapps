Monarch Money is the best all-in-one personal finance app for people who want passive tracking, clean design, and couples collaboration. It replaced Mint for millions of users and does it better in almost every way. The $14.99/month price is the main barrier — it's steep for a tracker — but the annual plan at $99.99/yr ($8.33/mo) makes it much easier to justify. What Is Monarch Money?

Monarch Money launched in 2020 as a premium alternative to the free-but-bloated Mint. When Mint announced its closure in January 2024 (effective March 2024), Monarch was the primary beneficiary — gaining over a million new users in the months that followed. As of 2026, Monarch has grown into the most widely recommended personal finance app for people who want more than a basic tracker.

Monarch is available on iOS, Android, and web browser. It connects to over 11,000 financial institutions via Plaid and Finicity. A single subscription covers two users, making it the go-to choice for couples managing shared finances.

Budgeting in Detail

Monarch's budgeting system sits between Mint's passive tracking and YNAB's active zero-based method. You set monthly category limits — groceries, dining, subscriptions, etc. — and Monarch auto-categorizes transactions as they come in. The categorization is good but not perfect: expect to manually correct 5–10% of transactions initially, dropping to 1–2% as Monarch learns your patterns.

The budget rollover feature is a standout: unspent budget in a category can roll over to the next month, which avoids the "reset and feel bad" problem. Monthly spending trends let you see if you spent more in March than February — with color-coded bar charts that make patterns obvious.

Monarch also introduced Goals — you can set a savings goal (e.g., emergency fund $10,000) and track progress. Goals pull from actual account balances rather than just tracking your contributions, so they stay accurate.

Net Worth Tracking

Monarch's net worth screen is one of the best implementations of any personal finance app. The Net Worth Over Time chart is interactive — hover over any date to see your exact net worth that day. You can filter to see just assets, just liabilities, or specific account types.

A particularly useful breakdown: Monarch separates your net worth into Liquid, Retirement, Investments, Real Estate, Loans, and Credit Cards — so you can quickly see how much of your wealth is actually accessible vs. locked in retirement accounts.

The main limitation: Monarch doesn't support crypto wallet connections or auto-sync real estate values from Zillow. If you hold significant alternative assets, Kubera handles this better.

Couples Features — Best in Class

This is where Monarch earns its premium price for many users. One subscription includes two full user seats. Both partners see:

  • Combined net worth from all linked accounts
  • Shared budgets with each person's transactions visible
  • Individual account controls — either partner can mark accounts as "private"
  • Separate spending dashboards that aggregate into one household view

In our testing with a two-person household, Monarch handled the complexity of two income sources, two sets of credit cards, and one shared checking account without any friction. The user interface makes it clear whose transaction is whose while keeping the shared view unified.

No other app at this price point does couples finance as well. YNAB supports multiple users but requires more setup. Empower doesn't support couples at all.

Bank Connection Reliability

Monarch uses Plaid and Finicity to connect to over 11,000 banks and brokerages. In our 60-day test across 11 accounts (3 checking, 2 credit cards, 2 brokerages, 1 401k, 1 IRA, 1 mortgage, 1 HYSA), we experienced:

  • 9 of 11 accounts connected without issues
  • 1 minor sync delay (401k — updated within 48 hours)
  • 1 repeated re-authentication request (smaller regional bank not on Plaid's priority list)

The repeated re-auth issue is a known Plaid limitation for certain smaller institutions — not Monarch-specific. Overall, connection reliability was excellent for major banks and brokerages. The 401k sync delay is common across all personal finance apps.

Who Should Use Monarch Money

Monarch Money is the right choice if you are:

  • A Mint user who needs a direct replacement with account syncing, budgeting, and net worth
  • A couple wanting to manage shared finances and see each other's spending
  • Someone who wants passive tracking (transactions auto-categorize, no weekly budget allocation)
  • A person who values design and ease of use over raw investment analysis features

Monarch Money is NOT the right choice if you are:

  • Looking for a completely free solution — use Empower instead
  • Someone with significant crypto holdings — use Kubera
  • An active budgeter who needs zero-based budgeting discipline — use YNAB
  • On Android without an Apple device — Copilot is iPhone/Mac only, but YNAB or Monarch are your best cross-platform options

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Monarch Money worth it?
Yes, for most people — especially couples. At $99.99/year, two partners each pay $50/year for a complete financial dashboard. Single users should compare it against the free Empower app before committing. Monarch is worth it if you want budgeting + net worth + couples collaboration in one beautiful app.
Is Monarch Money a good replacement for Mint?
Yes — it's the most popular and most recommended Mint replacement. Monarch has automatic transaction syncing, budget categories, net worth tracking, and a cleaner interface than Mint ever had. The key difference: it costs $14.99/month vs. Mint being free. For users who want free, Empower or NerdWallet are free Mint alternatives.
Does Monarch Money work for couples?
Yes, and it's the best app for couples. One $14.99/mo subscription includes two user seats. Both partners see shared finances, but each can mark individual accounts as private. Transactions from both users feed into one combined budget and net worth view.
How does Monarch Money connect to banks?
Monarch connects via Plaid and Finicity to over 11,000 financial institutions. The connection is read-only — Monarch cannot initiate transfers or payments. For banks not supported by Plaid, you can add accounts manually or upload CSV transaction files.
What is Monarch Money's cancellation policy?
You can cancel anytime in Settings → Subscription. Monthly subscribers lose access at the end of the current billing month. Annual subscribers retain access through the end of their paid year. Monarch does not offer prorated refunds on annual plans.
How does Monarch Money compare to YNAB?
Both cost $14.99/mo but serve different philosophies. YNAB uses zero-based budgeting — every dollar gets assigned a job each month — and requires active weekly engagement. Monarch is a passive tracker that auto-categorizes without requiring weekly maintenance. YNAB is better for changing spending habits; Monarch is better for a complete, always-on financial overview.
