Fidelity Review: Best Overall Brokerage

Fidelity has earned its reputation as the best all-around brokerage. In 2018, it made history by launching the world's first zero-expense-ratio index funds — FZROX (US Total Market), FZILX (International), FXNAX (US Bonds), and FZIPX (Extended Market) — each with a 0.00% expense ratio. This means Fidelity takes no cut of your investment returns whatsoever on these funds.

Beyond cost leadership, Fidelity excels on breadth. The mobile app is regularly rated the best in the industry. The research platform includes analysis from 20+ independent providers. Cash management earns competitive yields through the core money market position (SPAXX). And customer service — phone, chat, or in-person at 200+ branches — is consistently rated among the best in the industry.

The FZROX advantage

FZROX — Fidelity's Zero Total Market Index Fund — charges 0.00% and requires no minimum investment. Vanguard's equivalent, VTSAX, charges 0.04% and requires a $3,000 minimum. On a $100,000 portfolio, that's a $40/year difference in fees — not life-changing, but meaningful compounded over decades. The catch: FZROX is exclusive to Fidelity accounts and cannot be transferred out as shares (it would be liquidated).

Vanguard Review: Best for Index Fund Purists

Jack Bogle founded Vanguard in 1975 and invented the index fund as an investment product. Vanguard's unique structure — owned by the funds themselves, which are owned by the investors — means it has no outside shareholders to profit from. Every dollar saved on fees goes directly back to investors. This structural alignment of interests is genuinely unique in the financial industry.

Vanguard's funds — VTI, VTSAX, VTIAX, BND — are the gold standard of index investing. They're available at every brokerage, but you get the lowest expense ratios by holding them directly at Vanguard. The Vanguard platform has historically been criticized for its older interface, though significant improvements have been made in recent years.

Who Vanguard is best for

  • Long-term buy-and-hold investors who will never actively trade
  • Investors who specifically want to hold Vanguard's flagship funds at their lowest cost
  • Retirement investors who value Vanguard's mission-driven, investor-owned structure
  • People who don't need a modern app or active trading tools

Schwab Review: Best for Active Traders

Charles Schwab acquired TD Ameritrade in 2020 and inherited thinkorswim — widely considered the most powerful desktop trading platform available to retail investors. Schwab now offers thinkorswim alongside its own platform, giving active traders professional-grade charting, options analysis, futures trading, and paper trading tools that rival institutional platforms.

Schwab also has the largest branch network of the three brokerages (400+ locations), making it the best choice for investors who want in-person financial guidance. Its customer service and financial advisory services are consistently rated among the best in the industry.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Fidelity, Vanguard, or Schwab best in 2026?▼
Fidelity is the best overall brokerage for most investors in 2026 — it has zero-commission trades, zero-expense-ratio index funds (FZROX at 0.00%), excellent research tools, a top-rated mobile app, and no account minimum. Vanguard is best for pure buy-and-hold investors who want access to its legendary low-cost funds. Schwab is best for active traders who want thinkorswim and in-person branch access.
Which brokerage has the lowest fees?▼
All three charge $0 commissions on stock and ETF trades. For index fund expense ratios, Fidelity wins with its zero-expense-ratio funds: FZROX charges 0.00% with no minimum. Vanguard's VTI charges 0.03% and VTSAX charges 0.04%. Schwab's SCHB charges 0.03%. The cost difference between all three is negligible for most investors — the platform experience and features matter more.
Is Fidelity or Vanguard better for index funds?▼
Fidelity wins on expense ratios with FZROX (0.00%) vs Vanguard's VTSAX (0.04%) or VTI (0.03%). However, Fidelity's zero-fee funds are exclusive to Fidelity accounts and can't be transferred. If you plan to stay at one brokerage forever, Fidelity's zero-fee funds are cheaper. If flexibility to move your holdings matters, VTI (Vanguard ETF, available everywhere) is the more portable option.
Which brokerage is best for beginners?▼
Fidelity is the best brokerage for beginners in 2026. It has no account minimum, the best-rated mobile app, excellent free educational resources, fractional shares from $1, and the lowest-cost index funds available. Vanguard's interface can feel dated for newer investors. Schwab's thinkorswim platform can be overwhelming. Fidelity hits the right balance of powerful tools and approachable design for new investors.
What is the difference between VTSAX and FZROX?▼
Both VTSAX and FZROX track the total US stock market and have very similar performance. VTSAX (Vanguard) charges 0.04% with a $3,000 minimum investment and can be held at any brokerage. FZROX (Fidelity) charges 0.00% with no minimum — but it's exclusive to Fidelity accounts and cannot be transferred to another brokerage (it would need to be sold first). For investors committed to Fidelity long-term, FZROX is slightly cheaper. For flexibility and portability, VTSAX or VTI (the ETF version) is the better choice.
