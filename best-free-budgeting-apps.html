The 7 Best Free Budgeting Apps of 2026

#1 Best Free
Empower (formerly Personal Capital) 100% Free

Best for: complete financial dashboard — spending, net worth, and investments all tracked for free

Empower is the closest thing to a truly comprehensive free financial app. Originally launched as Personal Capital, it was acquired by Empower Retirement in 2020 and rebranded, but the core free personal finance tools remain intact and free. Connect your bank accounts, credit cards, and investment accounts, and Empower gives you a real-time view of your spending by category, your net worth trend, and how your investments are performing — all without paying a dollar.

  • Complete financial dashboard: spending, budgets, net worth, investment portfolio, and retirement projection — free
  • Investment performance tracking — see your portfolio's performance vs benchmarks
  • Retirement planner — projects whether you're on track for retirement based on current savings rate
  • Fee analyzer — scans your investment accounts for hidden fees and high-expense-ratio funds
  • No account minimum, no time limit, genuinely free with no subscription

Catch: Empower makes money by upselling its wealth management service ($89B+ AUM) to higher-net-worth users. Expect periodic calls or emails from financial advisors if your investable assets reach a certain level. These are easy to ignore, and the free tools remain free regardless.

#2
NerdWallet 100% Free

Best for: budget tracking + credit score monitoring in one free app

NerdWallet is most famous as a financial comparison site, but its app delivers a surprisingly solid free budgeting experience. Connect your accounts and it tracks spending by category automatically, shows your credit score (updated weekly), and monitors for unusual transactions. The "Cash Flow" tab gives a quick view of income vs expenses for the month.

  • Free credit score monitoring (updated weekly) — no credit card required
  • Automatic transaction categorization from connected bank and credit card accounts
  • Net worth tracker showing assets and liabilities over time
  • Personalized product recommendations (savings accounts, credit cards) based on your financial profile
Try NerdWallet Free →
#3
Rocket Money Free tier available

Best for: finding and canceling unwanted subscriptions — the free tier is legitimately useful

Rocket Money's standout feature is subscription detection: it scans your connected accounts and surfaces every recurring charge you're paying — including ones you've forgotten about. The free tier shows you all your subscriptions and lets you track spending. The premium features (bill negotiation, credit score, premium budget categories) require a paid plan starting at $6/month.

  • Finds ALL recurring subscriptions and charges automatically
  • Lets you cancel subscriptions directly through the app (premium feature)
  • Spending tracker with basic categories on the free tier
  • Bill negotiation service negotiates lower rates on your behalf (premium, keeps 40% of savings)
Try Rocket Money Free →
#4
Credit Karma 100% Free

Best for: credit score monitoring and credit card management

Credit Karma is primarily a credit score and credit monitoring app, but it includes basic spending tracking for connected accounts. With over 130 million members, it's the most widely used free financial app in the US. Its core strength is credit: free TransUnion and Equifax scores updated weekly, credit card utilization alerts, and personalized credit improvement tips.

  • Free credit scores from both TransUnion and Equifax, updated weekly
  • Credit monitoring with alerts for changes to your credit reports
  • Tax filing tool integrated (Credit Karma Tax, now owned by Cash App)
  • High-yield savings account through Credit Karma Money
Try Credit Karma Free →
#5
PocketGuard Free tier available

Best for: simple "how much can I safely spend today" awareness

PocketGuard's key feature is its "In My Pocket" calculation — it subtracts your bills, savings goals, and spending from your available money to show you exactly how much you can spend without jeopardizing your financial goals. The free tier supports connecting two bank accounts and includes basic budgeting categories. PocketGuard Plus ($7.99/month or $34.99/year) unlocks unlimited accounts and advanced features.

  • "In My Pocket" metric — clear, simple answer to "how much can I spend today?"
  • Bill tracking and subscription detection
  • Savings goal tracking built into the free tier
  • Simple, non-overwhelming interface — great for budgeting beginners
Try PocketGuard Free →
#6
Goodbudget Free tier (10 envelopes)

Best for: envelope budgeting method — free digital version of the cash envelope system

Goodbudget is a digital implementation of the envelope budgeting method — you allocate your income into "envelopes" (categories) at the start of each month and track spending against them. Unlike most apps, Goodbudget does not connect to your bank — you enter transactions manually. The free tier supports 10 envelopes and 1 device. Good for people who want a mindful, intentional budgeting approach without bank account linking.

  • Envelope budgeting method — highly effective for overspenders who want visual limits on each category
  • Syncs across devices so couples can budget together (even on the free tier)
  • No bank account connection required — works for people uncomfortable linking accounts
  • Clean, simple interface with no algorithmic upsells
Try Goodbudget Free →
#7
EveryDollar (Free Tier) Free tier (manual entry)

Best for: zero-based budgeting without paying for YNAB

EveryDollar is Dave Ramsey's budgeting app built on zero-based budgeting principles. The free tier lets you create a zero-based budget with manual transaction entry. The paid tier ($17.99/month or $79.99/year) adds automatic bank syncing — a significant limitation of the free version. But for people committed to manually tracking every dollar, the free tier delivers solid zero-based budgeting functionality.

  • Zero-based budgeting framework (give every dollar a job) — free
  • Clean monthly budget view with drag-and-drop category management
  • Dave Ramsey's Baby Steps integration for debt snowball tracking
  • Simple enough for complete budgeting beginners
Try EveryDollar Free →

When a Paid App Is Worth It

Free apps are a great starting point — but if you find yourself wanting automatic bank syncing, investment tracking, couples budgeting, or debt payoff tools, the paid apps are worth considering. YNAB ($99/year) and Monarch Money ($99/year) both offer free trials and deliver meaningfully more power than any free app. If a budgeting app helps you save even $200/year that you wouldn't have otherwise, it pays for itself twice over.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best completely free budgeting app in 2026?▼
Empower (formerly Personal Capital) is the best completely free budgeting app in 2026. It tracks spending, net worth, investment portfolio, and retirement readiness — all at no cost with no subscription required. NerdWallet is the runner-up, offering free spending tracking and credit score monitoring. Both are genuinely free with no hidden fees.
Is there a free Mint replacement?▼
The closest free replacement for Mint is Empower — it offers automatic transaction tracking, budget categories, net worth dashboard, and investment monitoring for free. NerdWallet also provides free spending tracking similar to Mint. For a more full-featured replacement including couples budgeting and deeper investment tracking, Monarch Money ($14.99/month) is the top choice but requires a paid subscription.
Are free budgeting apps safe to use?▼
Yes, the apps on this list are safe for connecting your financial accounts. They use read-only access via Plaid or similar aggregators — they can see your transaction history but cannot move money. They use bank-level 256-bit encryption and are regulated by relevant financial authorities. Empower, NerdWallet, Rocket Money, and Credit Karma are all established companies with millions of users. Always use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication on any financial app.
Does Rocket Money have a free version?▼
Yes. Rocket Money's free tier includes subscription tracking, basic spending visibility, and the ability to view all your recurring charges. It does not include bill negotiation, credit score monitoring, or the full premium feature set. Premium plans start at $6/month and add bill negotiation (Rocket Money keeps 40% of savings), unlimited budget categories, and credit score tracking.
